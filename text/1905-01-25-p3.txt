THE MAHMAL THEFT.

OFFICIAL VERSION.

I regard to the theft from the Mahmal's escort, we may state that the allegation in to-day's issue of a Cairo morning newspaper, that the case containing L.E. 8,000 in gold had after all been left in the Finance Ministry at Cairo, is quite untrue.

With regard to the theft, the following is the official version of the facts:

The money sent with the Mahmal was packed in twenty-two cases, three of which contained gold, the remainder silver coin. The packets were duly numbered and sealed at the Finance Ministry, and handed to over the “saraaf’ of the Mahmal caravan, who counted them, found them correct, and gave the receipt for them. They were then taken down to Abbassieh under guard, and put in a waggon belonging to the baggage train of the Mahmal, which started overnight for Suez. In a carriage next to the waggon containing the money travelled an escort of two officers and 6 men. The waggon had been duly locked and sealed, as soon as the money had been placed in it at Abbas-sieh, and at each station at which the train stopped the escort got down and formed up on each side of it. On reaching Genife station, just before Suez, the escort found one of the seals broken, and investigation at Suez revealed the fact that a case containing L.E. 8,000 in gold had disappeared.

How the money was removed from the train is a mystery. There was no foot-board, by which an agile thief could have reached the waggon while in motion, and the weight of the case being over 50 okes appears to preclude the possibility of only one thief having been concerned. It is also remarkable that the thieves were able to select one of the three cases containing gold. Of course, this may be a case of fortune having favored the enterprise.

Investigations are being made by the police and the Parquet into the matter, and we hear that Morice Bey is actively engaged on the spot. The whole case presents very extraordinary features, and the theft appears to bo the work of at least two very ingenious criminals.
